Bridgestone Americas, Inc.

I was a student corporate consultant for Bridgestone under Experential Learning Initiative by Krannert School of Management (Purdue). The R&D team at Ohio developed a new technology that could replace traditional air filled tires. Like many new products in technology, R&D comes before product management. The Research team wanted to present Airless tire technology to the business unit in Nashville and used ELI at Purdue to draw Product potential for Airless tires and for Bridgestone.

Roles and Achievements

• Studied the Traditional and Airless tire technology for all Tire applications.
• Formulated a framework to choose applications where the technology could be initially applied to.
• Conducted interviews and surveys to scope the demand and necessity of Airless tires. • Create marketing and adoption strategies based on purchasing and discovery patterns for chosen markets.
• Produced engineering design requirements for each market based on customer requirements.
